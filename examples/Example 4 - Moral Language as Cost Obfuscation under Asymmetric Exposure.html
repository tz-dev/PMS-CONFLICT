PMS-CONFLICT

Example 4: Moral Language as Cost Obfuscation under Asymmetric Exposure

Case label: Virtue Talk Where Exit Is Unequal Scope discipline: Structural mapping only. No diagnosis, no attribution, no prescriptions. PMS application validity remains conditional on Χ + reversibility + D.

A. Scene Packet (Minimal Unit)

Scene ID:

  • Θ (temporality): Ongoing, publicly visible interaction with cumulative records and reputational memory.

  • □ (frame anchor): □evaluation, presented as coordination (“doing the right thing together”), but operating as moral assessment.

  • Roles (not persons):

    • Role R₁: high exposure; statements and actions are durable, attributable, and publicly indexed.
    • Role R₂: low exposure; limited visibility, deniability of impact, and weak coupling to downstream consequences.

Trigger (Δ/∇):

  • Δ: Moral framing is introduced (e.g., “responsibility,” “care,” “integrity,” “doing what’s right”).
  • ∇: Pressure to align publicly and immediately with the moral frame.

Λ candidates (expected but missing):

  • Explicit calibration of exposure differences.
  • Explicit acknowledgment of unequal exit and revision costs.
  • Explicit separation between moral language and coordination interfaces.

Observed realizations (neutral):

  • Expansion of moral vocabulary.
  • Public alignment demands without exposure accounting.
  • Resistance to discussing asymmetry framed as moral evasion.

B. Drift Trace (Operator-Readable)

Ω (exposure asymmetry) → Φ_moral → Α_normative → Χ delegitimized → Ψ→Other → Σ displaced

Key transitions:

  • Ω (asymmetry) exists primarily as exposure and liability gradient.
  • Φ (recontextualization) converts structural differences into moral categories.
  • Α (attractor) stabilizes normative scripts (“good vs. irresponsible”).
  • Χ (distance) is delegitimized as lack of care or accountability.
  • Ψ (binding) is externalized: alignment is demanded from others.
  • Σ (integration) is displaced by moral compliance rather than coordination.

C. Cost Distribution (Structural, Not Moral)

Irreversible vs. relational costs:

  • R₁-adjacent costs: reputational exposure, durable attribution, escalation risk, long-term liability.
  • R₂-adjacent costs: minimal; moral claims carry leverage without equivalent exposure.

Cost asymmetry pattern:

  • Moral alignment is cheap for low-exposure roles.
  • Moral non-alignment is expensive for high-exposure roles.

System effect:

  • Moral language redistributes costs while presenting itself as neutral or elevating.

D. Rational Response Envelope (Structural Reasonableness)

Under this configuration, the following responses are structurally rational (not normatively preferred):

  • Public alignment without private coordination where dissent would amplify exposure.
  • Silence or minimal compliance where explicit refusal would escalate liability.
  • Withdrawal from substantive coordination while maintaining moral appearance.

These responses follow cost logic under Ω across Θ with moral Φ, not virtue or conviction.

E. Dignity-in-Practice Stress Test (D under Φ/Ω)

Risk point:

  • Moral language functions as enforcement without acknowledging exposure gradients.

Failure mode:

  • Χ reframed as coldness, indifference, or lack of values.
  • Ψ→Other: “You must align because this is the right thing.”

Structural indicator:

  • Naming asymmetry is treated as moral failure rather than structural clarification.

F. Reader-Guard (Misinterpretation Prevention)

This example does not claim:

  • that moral language is illegitimate,
  • that care or responsibility are undesirable,
  • that alignment implies insincerity,
  • that asymmetry implies bad faith.

It does claim:

  • that moral framing can function as a cost-obscuring substitute for coordination,
  • that exposure asymmetry persists beneath virtue talk,
  • that coercion can operate without explicit force.

G. PMS Validity Check (Application Gate)

Any attempt to use this analysis is PMS-valid only if:

  • Χ (distance) can be activated without moral sanction,
  • Ω (exposure asymmetry) is explicitly nameable,
  • binding remains Ψ→Self, not enforced Ψ→Other,
  • D (dignity-in-practice) constrains shaming and ranking.

Absent these conditions, application is structurally invalid.

H. Structural Closure (Non-Normative)

When moral language replaces exposure accounting under Ω across Θ, asymmetry does not disappear. It becomes unspeakable.

What appears as shared values functions as unequal risk allocation. What appears as care functions as pressure to absorb exposure. What appears as consensus functions as preemptive compliance.

At this point, coordination no longer negotiates costs. It moralizes them.

Conflict stabilizes not because values differ, but because virtue talk suppresses the only interface where asymmetry could still be coordinated.

Reading discipline: This signature marks a terminal conflict configuration where:

  • asymmetry is real but unnameable,
  • moral recontextualization replaces coordination,
  • binding is imposed via values,
  • distance is morally penalized.

J. Conflict Domain Classification (PMS-CONFLICT)

Domain status: CONFLICT (terminal descriptive regime)

Justification:

  • No internal reintegration grammar remains available.
  • Further moral clarification increases pressure without restoring Σ.
  • Resolution requires downstream mode shifts, not better moral articulation.

K. Asymmetry Lock-In Analysis (Ω × Θ)

Asymmetry form:

  • Differential exposure to public consequence and reputational persistence.

Lock-in mechanism:

  • Under Θ, moral records harden and become irreversible.
  • Under Ω, those with lower exposure can escalate moral demands cheaply.
  • The system stabilizes around moral enforcement as the least costly control mechanism.

L. χ-Availability Test (Distance Under Load)

Formal status: Χ exists Effective status: Χ is delegitimized

Indicators:

  • Pausing is framed as moral failure.
  • Contextualization is treated as excuse-making.
  • Distance increases rather than reduces exposure.

M. Tragedy Clause (Non-Correctability)

This configuration is tragic in the PMS sense:

  • No clarification of values restores coordination.
  • Moral escalation replaces integrative work permanently.
  • Any correction requires exit, governance, or institutional mediation.

N. Terminal Structural Closure (PMS-CONFLICT)

When moral language suppresses exposure accounting, conflict stabilizes through virtue-based coercion.

What fails is not ethics. What fails is coordination under unequal cost.

This outcome does not depend on bad intentions. It follows predictably whenever Ω is denied, Φ moralizes, Χ is penalized, and Σ is no longer affordable.
